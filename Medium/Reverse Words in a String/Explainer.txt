💡 Algorithm Explanation

🧠 Intuition
The goal is to reverse the order of words in the string while removing extra spaces.
Instead of manually handling spaces, we can take advantage of Python’s split() function,
which automatically separates words and ignores multiple spaces.

Once we have a clean list of words, reversing their order and joining them back
gives the desired result.

🛠️ Approach
1. Use split() to convert the string into a list of words.
   - This automatically removes leading, trailing, and extra spaces.
2. Reverse the list of words using slicing [::-1].
3. Join the reversed words using " ".join() to form the final string.

⏱️ Complexity
- Time Complexity: O(n)
  Each operation (split, reverse, join) processes the string once.

- Space Complexity: O(n)
  Extra space is used to store the list of words and the final result.

💭 Note:
This solution leverages Python’s built-in functions for simplicity and readability.
An in-place solution without extra space would require a more complex two-pointer approach.
